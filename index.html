Ancient Heritage

The Aravali Range is a mountain range in Northwestern India, running approximately 670 km in a southwest direction, starting near Delhi, passing through southern Haryana and Rajasthan, and ending in Gujarat.

Flora and Fauna

Despite the semi-arid climate, the hills are home to leopards, striped hyenas, and golden jackals. It acts as a green lung for the National Capital Region (NCR).
